Latest World News - June 15, 2026

1. U.S.-Iran Peace Deal:
A historic framework agreement has been reached between the United States and Iran to end the months-long war that broke out in late February. The deal is set to be formally signed in Geneva on June 19, 2026. The agreement includes the immediate and permanent end to military operations, the release of frozen Iranian assets, and compensation for war damages. While global leaders have welcomed the news as a significant step toward peace, Iran's foreign ministry has expressed that "deep mistrust" of the U.S. remains.

2. 52nd G7 Summit:
The G7 Summit has officially begun at a French lakeside resort, running from June 15 to 17, 2026. The summit is taking place under a significant geopolitical spotlight following the announcement of the U.S.-Iran peace deal, with leaders expected to seek common ground.

3. Ukraine-Russia War:
President Volodymyr Zelenskyy reported that nighttime bombardments by Russian forces targeted Kyiv and Kharkiv on June 15, resulting in at least 11 deaths and damage to a religious site. These attacks followed separate phone calls between U.S. President Donald Trump and both President Zelenskyy and Russian President Putin on June 14.

5. 2026 FIFA World Cup:
